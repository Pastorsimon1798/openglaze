## 2. Classic Glaze Families

### Shino
- **Origin:** Japan, Mino region
- **Characteristics:** Orange/white crawling surface, carbon trapping
- **Visual ID:** Distinctive crawling texture, orange to white color range
- **Chemistry:** High soda feldspar, low clay
- **Studio Examples:** Luster Shino, Malcom's Shino

### Celadon
- **Origin:** China, Korea
- **Characteristics:** Pale green/blue translucent, jade-like
- **Visual ID:** Transparent green/blue, visible clay body beneath
- **Chemistry:** Iron oxide in reduction (small amount 1-2%)
- **Studio Examples:** Ming Green, Celadon, Amber Celadon

### Chun (Blue Flambe)
- **Origin:** China
- **Characteristics:** Blue with purple/red edges, pooling
- **Visual ID:** Blue with color breaks at edges
- **Chemistry:** Copper and iron oxide combination
- **Studio Examples:** Chun Blue

### Tenmoku/Temmoku
- **Origin:** Japan (from Chinese Jian ware)
- **Characteristics:** Dark brown/black with amber/rust runs
- **Visual ID:** Dark base with lighter runs, oil spots possible
- **Chemistry:** High iron (8-10%)
- **Studio Examples:** Tenmoku

### Copper Red (Oxblood/Sang de Boeuf)
- **Origin:** China
- **Characteristics:** Bright red in reduction, can have blue highlights
- **Visual ID:** Red ranging from tomato to blood red
- **Chemistry:** Copper carbonate (0.5-1.5%) in heavy reduction
- **Studio Examples:** John's Red, Pablo's Red

### Oribe
- **Origin:** Japan
- **Characteristics:** Copper green/emerald
- **Visual ID:** Green ranging from bright to dark
- **Chemistry:** Copper in oxidation areas
- **Studio Examples:** None in current inventory

### Tea Dust
- **Origin:** China
- **Characteristics:** Dark base with green to gold spots when thick
- **Visual ID:** Black/dark brown with metallic crystalline spots
- **Chemistry:** High iron with specific cooling cycle
- **Studio Examples:** Teadust

## 6. Chemistry Notes

### Unity Molecular Formula (UMF)
**Definition:** Also called Seger Formula - an oxide formula normalized so that flux oxides (RO/R2O) total 1.0

**Purpose:**
- Standardizes glaze formulas for comparison across different recipes and materials
- Reveals the true oxide balance that determines melting behavior
- Essential for glaze stability and predicting defects (crazing, shivering)
- Required for material substitutions without changing glaze behavior
- Foundation for limit formulas and target ranges

**Key Ratios:**

#### Silica:Alumina Ratio
- **Glossy surfaces:** ~9:1 or higher (with adequate temperature)
- **Stable stoneware:** 5:1 to 7:1
- **Matte surfaces:** 3:1 to 5:1
- **Underfired:** below 3:1

**Importance:** Primary determinant of surface character and melt fluidity

### Molecular Formulas

#### Basic Materials
| Material | Formula | Role | Molecular Weight |
|----------|---------|------|------------------|
| Silica | SiO2 | Glass former - creates the main glass structure | 60.1 |
| Alumina | Al2O3 | Stabilizer - thickens and stabilizes melt, prevents running | 101.8 |
| Whiting (Calcium Carbonate) | CaCO3 | Flux - high temperature flux, hardens glaze | 100.1 |
| Dolomite | CaMg(CO3)2 | Flux - provides both CaO and MgO | 184.4 |
| Talc | Mg3Si4O10(OH)2 | Flux and opacifier - contributes MgO and SiO2 | 379.3 |

#### Feldspars
| Material | Formula | Role |
|----------|---------|------|
| Potash Feldspar | K2O·Al2O3·6SiO2 | Flux, alumina, and silica source |
| Soda Feldspar | Na2O·Al2O3·6SiO2 | Flux, alumina, and silica source |
| Nepheline Syenite | (Na,K)AlSiO4 | Sodium-rich feldspar substitute |

**Potash Feldspar Varieties:** Custer, G-200, K200, Primas P
**Soda Feldspar Varieties:** Kona F-4, Nepheline Syenite 270x/400x, Calspar

#### Common Colorants
| Material | Formula | Role | Oxide Content |
|----------|---------|------|---------------|
| Copper Carbonate | CuCO3 | Colorant - greens, blues, reds (atmosphere dependent) | ~64-65% CuO |
| Copper Oxide | CuO | Colorant - greens, blues, reds (atmosphere dependent) | ~100% CuO |
| Iron Oxide Red | Fe2O3 | Colorant - browns, grays, greens, yellows | ~95% Fe2O3 |
| Cobalt Carbonate | CoCO3 | Colorant - powerful blue colorant | ~63% CoO |
| Cobalt Oxide | Co3O4 | Colorant - most potent blue colorant | ~93% CoO |
| Manganese Dioxide | MnO2 | Colorant - purples and browns | ~100% MnO (effective) |
| Manganese Carbonate | MnCO3 | Colorant - purples and browns | ~61-62% MnO |
| Rutile | TiO2·Fe2O3 | Colorant/opacifier - tans, browns, blues | Titanium and iron combination |

### Material Substitutions

#### One-to-One Substitutions

##### Cobalt Oxide ↔ Carbonate
- **Co3O4 to CoCO3 ratio:** 1 : 1.48
- **CoCO3 to Co3O4 ratio:** 1 : 0.68
- **Note:** Cobalt oxide is 93% CoO; carbonate is 63% CoO

##### Copper Oxide ↔ Carbonate
- **CuO to CuCO3 ratio:** 1 : 1.55
- **CuCO3 to CuO ratio:** 1 : 0.64
- **Note:** Copper oxide is 100% CuO; carbonate is 64-65% CuO

##### Manganese Oxide ↔ Carbonate
- **MnO2 to MnCO3 ratio:** 1 : ~1.62
- **MnCO3 to MnO2 ratio:** 1 : ~0.62
- **Note:** MnO2 is 100% MnO; carbonate is 61-62% MnO

##### Whiting ↔ Wollastonite
- **CaCO3 to CaSiO3 ratio:** 1 : ~1.16
- **CaSiO3 to CaCO3 ratio:** 1 : ~0.86
- **Note:** Whiting is 56% CaO; Wollastonite is 48% CaO + 52% SiO2
- **Adjustment:** Reduce silica by ~6% when substituting wollastonite for whiting

##### Barium ↔ Strontium Carbonate
- **BaCO3 to SrCO3 ratio:** 1 : ~0.75
- **SrCO3 to BaCO3 ratio:** 1 : ~1.33
- **Note:** BaCO3 is 77.5% BaO; SrCO3 is 70% SrO
- **Safety Note:** Strontium is less toxic than barium

##### Red ↔ Yellow Iron Oxide
- **Yellow to Red ratio:** 1 : 0.93
- **Red to Yellow ratio:** 1 : 1.08
- **Note:** Yellow is 88% Fe2O3; Red is 95% Fe2O3

#### Complex Substitutions

##### Dolomite Replacement
- **Original:** Dolomite (CaMg(CO3)2)
- **Substitute:** Equal parts whiting + magnesium carbonate
- **Example:** 10% dolomite → 5% whiting + 5% magnesium carbonate
- **Note:** Close match but not exact due to trace elements

##### Feldspar Substitutions
- **Principle:** Choose from same group (potash, soda, or lithium)
- **Potash Group:** Custer, G-200, K200, Primas P
- **Soda Group:** Kona F-4, Nepheline Syenite, Calspar, NC-4
- **Note:** Nepheline syenite is NOT a direct substitute for potash feldspar

##### Gerstley Borate Replacement
- **Original:** Gerstley Borate (calcium borate ore)
- **Substitutes:** Colemanite (calcium borate), Boron frits (e.g., Ferro 3134), Borax (soluble - use calcined borax)
- **Note:** Both Gerstley Borate and Colemanite are variable

##### Albany Slip Replacements
- **Original:** Albany Slip (no longer mined)
- **Substitutes:** Alberta Slip, Seattle Slip, Sheffield Slip Clay Formula, A.R.T. Albany Slip Synthetic
- **Note:** Matching depends on amount in recipe, temperature, and atmosphere

### Target Ranges by Temperature

#### Cone 8-10 High Fire
| Surface Type | Alumina Range | Silica Range |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| Glossy | 0.3 - 0.6 | 3.0 - 6.0 |
| Satin | 0.35 - 0.6 | 2.0 - 5.0 |
| Matte | 0.35 - 0.8 | 2.0 - 5.0 |

**Note:** Wider silica ranges possible at high temperatures

#### Flux Ratio Interpretation
- **High Alkali (0.3+):** Higher expansion, more fluid, prone to crazing
- **Balanced (0.2-0.3):** Common for stable glazes
- **High Alkaline Earth (0.8+):** Lower expansion, stiffer melt, promotes mattes

**Importance:** Critical for thermal expansion and glaze fit

## 7. Firing Requirements

### Cone Temperature Chart

| Cone | Slow Ramp (60°C/hr) | Fast Ramp (150°C/hr) | Typical Use |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| 06 | 995°C (1823°F) | 1011°C (1852°F) | Standard bisque firing |
| 04 | 1060°C (1940°F) | 1070°C (1958°F) | Low-fire glaze temperature |
| 6 | 1220°C (2228°F) | 1241°C (2266°F) | Mid-range glaze firing |
| 10 | 1249-1305°C (2284-2381°F) | High-fire reduction glaze, porcelain |

**Note:** Temperatures vary by heating rate - slower ramps require lower temperatures due to heatwork (time + temperature)

### Reduction vs Oxidation

#### Oxidation Firing
- **Definition:** Firing technique where oxygen is freely available to interact with clay and glazes
- **Visual Characteristics:** Blue flame, clear kiln atmosphere, roaring sound
- **Effects on Glazes:**
  - Bright, saturated glaze colors
  - Deep brick red clay bodies
  - Complete carbon burn-out from raw clay
  - White glazes stay pure white
  - Intensified primary and secondary colors

#### Reduction Firing (Studio Process)
- **Definition:** Kiln firing technique where oxygen is partially restricted, creating a reducing atmosphere
- **Visual Characteristics:** Lengthy, gentle orange or yellow flames, murky kiln air, orange/yellow flames at damper and peepholes
- **Effects on Glazes:**
  - Muted and grayed glaze colors
  - Darkened clay bodies
  - Increased metallic luster in glazes
  - Reduction speckling and flashing
  - Rich dark browns and blacks
  - Metallic luster in copper and iron glazes
  - Carbon trapping in white glazes (shino magic)

### Reduction Schedule

#### Initial Oxidation
- **Range:** Room temperature to Cone 010 (~898°C / 1648°F)
- **Purpose:** Prepare work, complete early oxidation
- **Characteristics:** Blue flame, clear atmosphere

#### Reduction Phase
- **Range:** Cone 010 to Cone 9 (~898°C to 1257°C / 1648°F to 2295°F)
- **Purpose:** Create reduction effects in clay body and glazes
- **Characteristics:** Orange/yellow flames, murky atmosphere
- **Body Reduction:** Cone 012-08 for clay body effects
- **Carbon Trap Shinos:** Start around 850°C (1562°F) when soda begins to melt
- **Maintenance:** Neutral to slightly reducing atmosphere until cone 9

#### Final Oxidation (Optional)
- **Range:** Cone 9 to Cone 10 (final 20 minutes)
- **Purpose:** Smoother, brighter glaze surfaces
- **Characteristics:** Return to oxidizing atmosphere

### Typical Cone 10 Reduction Schedule

| Step | Rate (°F/hr) | Rate (°C/hr) | Target (°F) | Target (°C) | Hold | Purpose |
|------|--------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|------|---------|
| 1 | 150 | 83 | 250 | 121 | 0 | Gentle heating, prevent thermal shock |
| 2 | 350 | 194 | 2050 | 1121 | 0 | Ramp through quartz inversion and early stages |
| 3 | 120 | 66 | 2250 | 1232 | 0 | Slower approach to peak temperature |
| 4 | 60 | 33 | 2290 | 1254 | 0 | Very slow approach to final temperature |
| 5 | 40 | 22 | 2315 | 1268 | 15 | Peak temperature hold for glaze maturity |
| 6 | 400 | 222 | 1700 | 926 | 90 | Controlled cooling for crystal development and stress relief |

**Estimated Time:** 12-16 hours depending on kiln size and loading

### Critical Temperatures
- **Boiling Point:** 100°C / 212°F - water must evaporate slowly
- **Quartz Inversion:** 573°C / 1063°F - structural change in clay, causes stress
- **Carbon Burnout:** 400-700°C - organic materials burn out
- **Cristobalite Conversion:** 225°C / 437°F - avoid rapid cooling

### Kiln Atmosphere Monitoring

#### Oxidation Indicators
- Blue flames
- Clear atmosphere
- Roaring sound
- No back pressure

#### Reduction Indicators
- Orange/yellow flames
- Murky atmosphere
- Back pressure at peepholes
- Black smoke (too much reduction)

#### Neutral Indicators
- Greenish flames

### Clay Body Effects

**Buff/Tosty Stoneware (Left Tiles):**
- Warmer undertones
- Glazes interact with iron in body
- Carbon trapping more visible
- Breaking effects more pronounced

**White Stoneware/Porcelain (Right Tiles):**
- Cooler, cleaner colors
- Glazes sit on top without body interaction
- Translucency more apparent
- Celadons show clearer greens

### Application Thickness Effects

**Pooling (Darker in Recesses):**
- Ming Green - Extreme
- Amber Celadon - Dramatic tobacco/straw
- Celadon family - Subtle
- Larry's Grey - Noticeable

**Breaking (Color Shift at Edges):**
- Tenmoku - Rust/brown at edges
- Amber Celadon - Straw on ridges
- Jensen Blue - Brown breaking
- John's Red - Possible green at edges (copper re-oxidation)

**Stable/No Movement:**
- Long Beach Black - Saturated
- Choinard White - Opaque
- Mellow Yellow - Stable matt

### Reduction Atmosphere Effects Confirmed

1. **Carbon Trapping:** Malcolm's Shino, Luster Shino (black speckles in white/orange)
2. **Oil Spots:** Tenmoku, Angel Eyes, Oilspot Blue/Green (metallic spots)
3. **Celadon Development:** Ming Green, Celadon, Amber Celadon (green from iron reduction)
4. **Copper Red:** John's Red, Pablo's Red (metallic copper red in reduction)
5. **Micro-crystallization:** Cosmic Brown, Teadust, Strontium Crystal Magic
6. **Rayleigh Scattering:** Chun Blue (opalescent milky depth from micro-bubbles)
