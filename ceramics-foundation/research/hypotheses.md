## Glaze Chemistry

### H-001: SiO2:Al2O3 thresholds for surface prediction
- **Hypothesis:** The thresholds high_gloss=10, glossy=7, satin=4, matte=1.5 better predict surface character than the previous thresholds (9, 5, 3, 1.5)
- **Confidence:** Medium — based on Stull chart interpretation and Digitalfire references, but needs empirical testing with known glazes
- **Date:** 2026-03-17
- **Sources:** Stull chart, Digitalfire articles on surface prediction

### H-002: Base chemistry influence on iron color
- **Hypothesis:** Ca-heavy bases push iron toward celadon/yellow-green; K-heavy bases allow deeper greens in reduction
- **Confidence:** High — well-documented in ceramic literature
- **Date:** 2026-03-17
- **Sources:** Digitalfire, Hamer & Hamer "The Potter's Dictionary"

### H-003: Copper red failure rate
- **Hypothesis:** Copper reds have ~50-60% failure rate for beginners, improving to 20-40% with experience
- **Confidence:** Medium — commonly cited but varies significantly with base chemistry, firing schedule, and kiln
- **Date:** 2026-03-17
- **Sources:** Studio testing, community reports
